About University of Bristol

The University of Bristol is a public university established in 1876, in Bristol, UK, as a University College. The University has a student enrollment of 29,434 students from various nationalities. The university is known for its state-of-the-art research facility and high-quality student education. The Bristol University campus is in multiple locations. The main Bristol Campus is spread over a large area across the city centre, interconnected by bus routes. Each campus has its unique characteristics and facilities. The university offers more than 281 undergraduate and 230 postgraduate majors.

As per major ranking bodies, the University of Bristol ranking is among the top 100 universities in the world and ranks #54 among global universities. Shiksha popularity Rankings for 2025, ranks Bristol University at #47 in world.

University of Bristol History

The University of Bristol is one of the popular universities across the UK. The university was formed in 1909 after the merger of University College Bristol and Merchant Ventures Technical College. The university was given the Royal Charter in 1909 officially establishing it as a university. During the World Wars, the university played a crucial role in the training and management of the military. In the era of sustainable development, the university earned its reputation through its extensive research and focus on making the world a better place. The university has had a great influence on the social issues of the locals.

Q:   Is it hard to get into the University of Bristol?
A: 

Bristol University admission is competitive because of its low acceptance rate. The Uni of Bristol acceptance rate is 46%, that means out of 100 applications received at the university only 46 are accepted by the university. International students are also required to meet the admission criteria and submit all the required documents to get into the university successfully.

Q:   Is Bristol expensive for international students?
A: 

 Bristol is less expensive than other cities in the UK like London, Cambridge, Oxford, etc. The cost of living in the UK in Bristol is around £984.29 (avg. 1 B.Ed Rental in City Centre (PCM) by essentialliving.co.uk. The total expense depends on the living cost and tuition fee of the programme that students select to study at Bristol University. Living expenses cover everything you pay except your tuition fees. Most students spend between £9,000 and £15,000 per year including accommodation. Also check, the Cost of Living for International students in the UK
